“Dark Data” Composting Platform

1. Challenge

Companies store huge amounts of unused data — old logs, duplicated files, stale backups, obsolete images, and redundant analytics tables. This “dark data” consumes storage, energy, cooling, and hardware. It contributes to emissions and critical material demand while adding cybersecurity and compliance risk.

2. Specific sub-group, sector, trends, unmet need

Sub-group: unstructured enterprise data waste.
Sector: digital infrastructure → cloud storage → enterprise data governance.
Trends: data volumes are growing faster than companies’ ability to classify and delete data. Your research highlights data-center energy use and critical raw materials as key challenge areas.  
Unmet need: companies are afraid to delete data, even when it has no business value.

3. Proposed solution and innovation

A “data composting” system that classifies old data into: keep, compress, anonymize, archive, sell as synthetic insights, or delete. It gives each dataset a carbon storage cost and a legal-risk score.
Innovation: converts data deletion into an ESG action with auditable carbon savings.

4. Target market and product-market fit

Primary market: enterprises with large cloud bills: finance, telecom, e-commerce, health, media, cyber, and SaaS companies.
Segments:

* Cloud storage optimization.
* Legal/compliance.
* ESG reporting.
* Cybersecurity.
    Product-market fit: strong because storage reduction saves money and reduces risk.

5. Existing solutions and deficiencies

Existing solutions: data catalogues, storage-tiering, backup software, cloud cost management tools.
Deficiencies: they optimize price but rarely frame deletion as environmental impact with audit-ready reporting.
Major players: AWS S3, Google Cloud Storage, Azure Blob, Snowflake, Databricks, Rubrik, Veeam.
Gap: lack of a simple “this terabyte is useless and has a carbon/legal cost” decision engine.

Guidelines fit

Problem and solution are easy to present. It works by scanning metadata, access frequency, duplication, sensitivity, and storage location. It serves cloud-heavy enterprises. Feasibility is high because the data already exists. Market sectors are cloud storage, data governance, ESG analytics. Existing solutions are fragmented and not ESG-first.
